title: Learning the USM method through the story of Maria
client: onITnow
project: The story of Maria
sector: IT services and service management
doelgroep: New and existing service employees
vorm: MicroGame Story with a comic and five MicroGames
rol: Story design and development
summary: >-
  Maria works at a municipal service desk and is drowning in ever-new requests. Her search for
  calm and control is the heart of a MicroGame Story that introduces the USM method — learning
  through a recognisable story instead of dry theory.
highlights:
  - A recognisable storyline that makes workload and stress tangible
  - Five MicroGames for core knowledge and practical application
  - Self-paced, around 120 minutes
uitdaging: >-
  Maria and her colleagues are constantly confronted with new requests, disrupting their work and
  raising the pressure. How do you convey the principles of service management without slipping
  into dry, theoretical training?
aanpak: >-
  We developed a MicroGame Story as a core training and introduction to the USM method (Unified
  Service Management). A comic brings complex concepts — such as the Agree process, the ITOCO model
  and the difference between facility, support and service — to life visually and accessibly, at
  the learner's own pace.
ervaring: >-
  Four MicroGames transfer core knowledge, a fifth offers practical examples. The combination of
  storytelling and interaction keeps participants engaged: they connect new knowledge to emotions
  and recognisable experiences and absorb the material in about 120 minutes, in their own time.
resultaat: >-
  An activating, dynamic and motivating training on the onITnow learning platform, helping both
  new and experienced employees understand and apply the principles of USM in their daily work.
inzicht: >-
  Storytelling is a fundamental part of learning: it provides context and meaning, so knowledge
  sticks better. Maria's story shows how a service-oriented approach eases workload and improves
  the quality of service.
order: 8

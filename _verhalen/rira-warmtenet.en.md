title: Experiencing the heat transition by making choices together
client: Amsterdam University of Applied Sciences
project: RiRa Heat Network Game
sector: Education and energy transition
jaar: 2021
doelgroep: Students, building owners and stakeholders in the heat transition
vorm: Decision and dialogue game (online and offline)
rol: Game design and development
summary: >-
  The Netherlands has to move away from natural gas, but choosing a heating solution is a complex
  interplay of factors and actors. The RiRa Heat Network Game makes those motivations and
  consequences visible by letting players make choices together.
highlights:
  - Making the motivations of different actors visible
  - Linking the consequences of choices to critical success factors
  - Playable both online (MIRO) and offline (board game)
uitdaging: >-
  In the switch from natural gas to alternatives for cooking, showering and heating, the choice
  does not rest with one person. Not all information is available to everyone, and each actor
  applies its own conditions. With a collective system, building owners must decide together where
  they would normally decide alone.
ontwerpvraag: >-
  How do we make the motivations of actors and the consequences of choices visible, so an open
  dialogue about the heating concept can get going?
aanpak: >-
  We developed a decision and dialogue game driven by playing cards. Three triggers spark the
  conversation: changing municipal objectives, choosing a personal critical success factor, and
  deciding which technical scenarios — collective or individual — get developed. A game leader
  guides the dialogue and gradually releases new information.
route: >-
  The game is based on the Amstel III case in Amsterdam Zuidoost, where waste heat from data
  centres makes a local heat network possible. Because of COVID, the game is playable both offline
  (a board game with cards) and online (via whiteboard software MIRO).
resultaat: >-
  The RiRa Heat Network Game is first used in education, where students explore positions and
  options. Project partners also see opportunities to develop the game further for real-world area
  cases.
inzicht: >-
  Triggers and a skilled game leader prompt players to act and reflect on the challenge. The game
  was developed by the Amsterdam University of Applied Sciences and Daan Groen within the RiRa
  research project.

Every time I work with a client, somewhere in the process I see a change in their eyes and posture. As if a light has been switched on. That is when I know they have found their game.

I wrote this text after meeting a friend. He was sharing his struggles with a concept at work and how hard it was to get it adopted. The thing was: he had never considered serious games.

## The assumption that is often wrong

My friend was familiar with my work as a game designer and had a positive view of serious games. Yet he never approached me — he assumed serious games applied to other situations. Training or team-building games, he thought, were more logical applications. In his case, a more nuanced change was needed among leaders, to think differently. And honestly, the old way was just easier. PowerPoints and phone calls. Those were the known mechanics.

I asked him how he could be so sure serious games wouldn't work — especially since serious gaming comes in many shapes and sizes. Had he ever considered a Jolt for his situation, for example?

## From scepticism to concrete options

This time we went deeper. I shared more examples, and soon we were brainstorming about a very simple yet effective serious game. Through that conversation he moved from sceptic to actionable options. His body language changed and he started smiling. *"You're right,"* he said, *"I think I've found my game."*

You'd be surprised how often I encounter this: people who react positively to serious games, yet assume it won't apply to their situation. And you'd be surprised how often that assumption is wrong.

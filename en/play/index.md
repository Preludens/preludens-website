    <h2>Play as a technique</h2>
    <p>
      At Preludens, we use Play to create an open mindset. People step into a safe environment with clear rules,
      where they can explore new roles, make mistakes, and gain meaningful experience.
    </p>
    <p>
      That connects with Johan Huizinga's <em>Homo Ludens</em>: play has structure, purpose, and clear boundaries.
      It orders experience and gives it meaning — exactly what intrinsic motivation and behaviour change need.
    </p>

    <h2>What Play delivers</h2>
    <ul>
      <li><strong>Safe experimentation</strong> — making mistakes without real-world damage</li>
      <li><strong>Active engagement</strong> — choosing, acting, immediate feedback</li>
      <li><strong>Recognisable context</strong> — storytelling that connects to the job</li>
      <li><strong>Measurable growth</strong> — data to improve with purpose</li>
    </ul>

    <p>
      Combined with MicroGames and narrative scenarios, learning stops being passive obligation
      and becomes an active experience where meaning, emotion, and application come together.
    </p>
